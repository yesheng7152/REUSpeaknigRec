'''Broad Creek''' is an unincorporated area and [[census-designated place]] (CDP) in [[Carteret County, North Carolina|Carteret County]], [[North Carolina]], United States. As of the [[2010 United States Census|2010 census]] it had a population of 2,334.<ref name="Census 2010">{{cite web| url=http://factfinder2.census.gov/bkmk/table/1.0/en/DEC/10_DP/G001/1600000US3708040| title=Geographic Identifiers: 2010 Demographic Profile Data (G001): Broad Creek CDP, North Carolina| publisher=U.S. Census Bureau, American Factfinder| accessdate=December 24, 2014}}</ref> It is part of the greater [[Newport, North Carolina|Newport]] area and currently shares the Newport zip code of 28570.

==Geography==
Broad Creek is located in west-central Carteret County, on the north shore of [[Bogue Sound]], between the tidal inlets of [[Broad Creek (Bogue Sound)|Broad Creek]] to the west and [[Gales Creek (North Carolina)|Gales Creek]] to the east. Bogue Sound is separated from the [[Atlantic Ocean]] to the south by [[Bogue Banks]], part of North Carolina's [[barrier island]]s known as the [[Crystal Coast]]. The Broad Creek community is bordered on the north by [[Croatan National Forest]].

[[North Carolina Highway 24]] passes through the community, leading east {{convert|12|mi}} to [[Morehead City, North Carolina|Morehead City]] and west {{convert|8|mi|0}} to [[Cape Carteret, North Carolina|Cape Carteret]]. Newport is {{convert|7|mi|0}} to the northeast up Hibbs Road. 9 Mile Road runs north from Broad Creek, providing a direct route to [[Havelock, North Carolina|Havelock]], {{convert|13|mi}} to the north. Broad Creek Loop Road forms a loop through the center of the community, south of NC 24.

The Broad Creek CDP has a total area of {{convert|8.1|km2|disp=flip}}, of which {{convert|0.03|km2|disp=flip|2}}, or 0.35%, is water.<ref name="Census 2010"/>

Housing developments in Broad Creek include Salty Shores, Croatan Colony, Rolling Wood, Fox Lair, Bogue Pines, Whispering Pines, Karobi Park, Adams Harbor, Pearson Circle, Bar Harbor, Bluewater Banks.
