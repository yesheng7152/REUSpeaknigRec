'''Mayfield''', also known as the William Wilson House, is a historic home located near [[Middletown, Delaware|Middletown]], [[New Castle County, Delaware]].  It was built about 1839, and is a 2 1/2-story, five bay, center hall plan brick dwelling with a two-story, rear service wing.  The house is in a vernacular [[Greek Revival architecture|Greek Revival]] style. It measures approximately 45 feet wide and 25 feet deep.  It features a steeply pitched gable roof with dormers and tetrastyle, Greek-Revival style porch on brick footings.<ref name=nrhpdoc>{{cite web|url=http://pdfhost.focus.nps.gov/docs/NRHP/Text/97000836.pdf |title=National Register of Historic Places Inventory/Nomination: Mayfield |author=Susan Brizzolara|date=April 1997 |publisher=National Park Service}} and [http://pdfhost.focus.nps.gov/docs/NRHP/Photos/97000836.pdf accompanying 11 photos]</ref>

It was listed on the [[National Register of Historic Places]] in 1997.<ref name = nris/>
