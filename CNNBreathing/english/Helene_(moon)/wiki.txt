'''Helene''' ({{pron-en|ˈhɛlɨniː}} {{respell|HEL|ə-nee}}, sometimes {{IPA-en|hɨˈliːniː|}} {{respell|hə|LEE|nee}}, or as in Greek ''Ἑλένη)'' is a [[natural satellite|moon]] of [[Saturn]]. It was discovered by [[Pierre Laques]] and [[Jean Lecacheux]] in 1980 from ground-based observations at [[Pic du Midi Observatory]], and was designated '''{{nowrap|S/1980 S 6}}.'''<ref>[http://cfa-www.harvard.edu/iauc/03400/03496.html IAUC 3496: ''Satellites of Saturn''] 1980 July 31 (discovery)</ref> In 1988 it was officially named after [[Helen]] of [[Troy]], who was the granddaughter of [[Cronus]] (Saturn) in [[Greek mythology]].<ref>[http://cfa-www.harvard.edu/iauc/04600/04609.html IAUC 4609: ''Satellites of Saturn and Uranus''] 1988 June 8 (naming the moon)</ref> The moon is also designated '''{{nowrap|Saturn XII}}''', a number which it received in 1982, under the designation '''Dione B''',<ref>Transactions of the International Astronomical Union, Vol. XVIIIA, 1982 <small>(mentioned in [http://cfa-www.harvard.edu/iauc/03800/03872.html IAUC 3872: ''Satellites of Jupiter and Saturn''], 1983 September 30)</small></ref> because it is co-orbital with [[Dione (moon)|Dione]] and located in its leading [[Lagrangian point]] ({{L4}}).

__NOTOC__

== Exploration ==
The closest images of Helene are from the [[Cassini-Huygens|Cassini]] spacecraft's 36,000&nbsp;km fly-by in 2007. Helene will be the target of a much closer fly-by (1800&nbsp;km) during Cassini's extended mission on 2010 March 3.
