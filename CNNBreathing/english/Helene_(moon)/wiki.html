Helene (pronounced /ˈhɛlɨniː/ (deprecated template) HEL-ə-nee, sometimes /hɨˈliːniː/ hə-LEE-nee, or as in Greek Ἑλένη) is a moon of Saturn. It was discovered by Pierre Laques and Jean Lecacheux in 1980 from ground-based observations at Pic du Midi Observatory, and was designated S/1980 S 6.[3] In 1988 it was officially named after Helen of Troy, who was the granddaughter of Cronus (Saturn) in Greek mythology.[4] The moon is also designated Saturn XII, a number which it received in 1982, under the designation Dione B,[5] because it is co-orbital with Dione and located in its leading Lagrangian point (L4).


Exploration[edit]

The closest images of Helene are from the Cassini spacecraft's 36,000 km fly-by in 2007. Helene will be the target of a much closer fly-by (1800 km) during Cassini's extended mission on 2010 March 3.
