'''Maisie Williams''' (born 15 April 1997)<ref>{{cite web|title=Maisie Williams|url=http://www.tv.com/maisie-williams/person/657601/biography.html|publisher=[[TV.com]]|accessdate=29 July 2011}}</ref> is an English actress and dancer. She is best known for her role as [[Arya Stark]] in the [[HBO]] television series ''[[Game of Thrones]]'', which earned her a [[Airlock Alpha|Portal Award for Best Supporting Actress – Television]] (2012), [[Airlock Alpha|Portal Award for Best Young Actor]] (2012), [[BBC| BBC Radio 1 Teen Award for Best British Actor]] (2013), [[2011 Scream Awards|Scream Award for Best Ensemble]] nomination (2011), and [[Young Artist Award|Young Artist Award for Best Performance in a TV Series - Supporting Young Actress]] nomination (2013).

==Early life==
Williams was born in [[Bristol]],<ref name="Bristol Post 23 March 2014">{{cite news|title=Bristol teenager Maisie Williams on leaving school at 14 to become star of Game of Thrones |url=http://www.bristolpost.co.uk/Bristol-teenager-Maisie-Williams-leaving-school/story-20843299-detail/story.html |accessdate=23 March 2014 |newspaper=[[Bristol Post]] |date=23 March 2014}}</ref> the youngest of four children of Hilary Williams (now Frances), a former university course administrator. She grew up in [[Somerset]].<ref name="Bristol Post 23 March 2014">{{cite news|title=Bristol teenager Maisie Williams on leaving school at 14 to become star of Game of Thrones|url=http://www.bristolpost.co.uk/Bristol-teenager-Maisie-Williams-leaving-school/story-20843299-detail/story.html|accessdate=23 March 2014|newspaper=[[Bristol Post]]|date=23 March 2014}}</ref> She attended [[Clutton, Somerset|Clutton]] Primary School and [[Norton Hill School]] in [[Midsomer Norton]] before moving to Bath Dance College to study Performing Arts.<ref name = "MeetMaisie">{{cite web|title=Meet Maisie Sean Bean's co-star in new TV series Game of Thrones |url=http://www.thisissomerset.co.uk/Maisie-starring-role-Sean-Bean/story-12340725-detail/story.html |date=27 January 2011 |work=thisissomerset.co.uk |accessdate=2 June 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=BDC Student Maisie Williams|url=http://www.bathdancecollege.com/videos/|accessdate=14 October 2013|work=Bath Dance College}}</ref>

==Career==
[[File:Maisie Williams (March 2013) (headshot).jpg|thumb|Williams at the premiere of the [[Game of Thrones (season 3)|third season]] of ''[[Game of Thrones]]'', March 2013]]

Since 2011, Williams has played [[Arya Stark]], a [[tomboy]]ish young girl from a noble family, in the [[HBO]] fantasy TV series ''[[Game of Thrones]]''.<ref name="TV Guide 2 June 2011">{{cite news|last=Nguyen|first=Hanh|title=Game of Thrones' Maisie Williams: I Did Try to Make Arya Left-Handed! |url=http://www.tvguide.com/News/Game-Thrones-Maisie-Williams-1033874.aspx?rss=keywords&partnerid=imdb&profileid=01|accessdate=29 July 2011|newspaper=[[TV Guide]]|date=2 June 2011}}</ref> It was her first role.<ref name="Access Hollywood 13 June 2011">{{cite news|last=Spero |first=Jesse|title=Maisie Williams Talks ''Game of Thrones'' Shocking Death|url=http://www.accesshollywood.com/maisie-williams-talks-game-of-thrones-shocking-death_article_49207|accessdate=29 July 2011|newspaper=[[Access Hollywood]]|date=13 June 2011}}</ref> She received acclaim for her performance.<ref>{{cite news|last=Porter|first=Rick|title=''Game of Thrones'' review: Well-acted, beautifully shot fantasy for grownups|url=http://blog.zap2it.com/frominsidethebox/2011/04/game-of-thrones-review-well-acted-beautifully-shot-fantasy-for-grownups.html|accessdate=29 July 2011|newspaper=Zap2It|date=17 April 2011}}</ref><ref name="The Telegraph 6 June 2011">{{cite news|last=Chivers|first=Tom|title=Game of Thrones, episode eight – The Pointy End, review|url=http://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/tvandradio/8560692/Game-of-Thrones-episode-eight-The-Pointy-End-review.html|accessdate=29 July 2011|newspaper=[[The Daily Telegraph|The Telegraph]]|date=6 June 2011}}</ref> Williams continued to garner praise in the show's [[Game of Thrones (season 2)|second season]]<ref>{{cite web|last=Sims |first=David|url=http://www.avclub.com/articles/the-ghost-of-harrenhal-for-newbies,72795/|title=“The Ghost of Harrenhal" (for newbies) &#124; Game of Thrones (newbies) &#124; TV Club &#124; TV|publisher=The A.V. Club|date=29 April 2012|accessdate=25 June 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.forbes.com/sites/erikkain/2012/04/30/game-of-thrones-sails-into-darker-waters-with-ghost-of-harrenhal/2/|title='Game of Thrones' Sails into Darker Waters With 'Ghost of Harrenhal'|publisher=Forbes|date=30 April 2012|accessdate=25 June 2013}}</ref> and HBO submitted her for consideration as [[Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Drama Series|Outstanding Supporting Actress]] at the [[64th Primetime Emmy Awards|2012 Primetime Emmy Awards]].<ref>{{cite news|first=Newsdesk|title=Let's peek inside HBO's Emmy FYC campaign package|url=http://www.goldderby.com/emmys/news/2774/let%27s-peek-inside-hbo%27s-emmy-fyc-campaign-package.html|accessdate=5 May 2012|newspaper=Gold Derby}}</ref> She won the 2012 [[Airlock Alpha|Portal Award]] for Best Supporting Actress {{ndash}} Television, and the Portal Award for Best Young Actor. At 15 years of age, Williams was the youngest actress ever to win the Best Supporting Actress category.<ref>Airlock Alpha. {{cite web|title='Game of Thrones' Conquers With 4 Portal Awards|url=http://www.airlockalpha.com/node/9361/game-of-thrones-conquers-with-4-portal-awards.html|accessdate= 19 September 2012}}</ref> In March 2013 she was nominated for a [[Young Artist Award]] as [[34th Young Artist Awards#Best Performance in a TV Series - Supporting Young Actress|Best Young Supporting Actress in a TV Series]] and, in November 2013, won a [[BBC Radio 1]] Teen Award for Best British Actor.<ref name="Young Artist Awards 34">{{cite web|url=http://www.youngartistawards.org/noms34.html|title=34th Annual Young Artist Awards|accessdate=31 March 2013|work=YoungArtistAwards.org}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=BBC Radio 1's Teen Awards, 2013|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/p01ktm3g.mp|accessdate=4 November 2013|publisher=BBC Radio 1}}</ref>

In 2012, Williams played Loren Caleigh in the [[BBC]] series ''[[The Secret of Crickley Hall (TV series)|The Secret of Crickley Hall]]'' and appeared in a ''[[Funny or Die]]'' skit titled ''The Olympic Ticket Scalper''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.funnyordie.com/videos/2d26100ce3/the-olympic-ticket-scalper-with-patrick-stewart-ryan-lochte-simon-pegg-maisie-williams|title=The Olympic Ticket and ''Up On The Roof'' (2013).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.louisejohnstonmanagement.com/showpage.asp|title=Louise Johnston Management|publisher=Louise Johnston Management|accessdate=25 June
